What is Entrepreneurship?

Entrepreneurship is the process of creating, launching and running a new business. It often starts as a small business. The people who create these businesses are called entrepreneurs.

An entrepreneur is a person who sets up and runs a business. Sometimes that person takes a big risk — and hopes that it will pay off!

In this lesson, you will learn about three entrepreneurs who made a big impact on Ghana and its people. 🌍

🧑‍💼 Meet the Entrepreneurs
🪵

George Grant

Timber Merchant & Founding Father
Born: 15 August 1878 · Beyin, Western Region
Also known asPaa Grant
BusinessTimber trade
Company founded1896
Company nameGeorge Grant & Company

George Alfred Grant was known as Paa Grant. He worked in the timber trade at a time when most timber companies in Ghana were owned by Europeans.

In 1896, he set up his own company — George Grant and Company. He became a successful timber merchant with an export business, sending timber to other countries.

Paa Grant did not just think about business. He also worked hard to develop his community. He helped bring street lighting and pipe-borne water to Sekondi and Axim — something that changed everyday life for the people there.

George Grant's grandson, Kim Tyrone Grant, was a national player for the Ghana Black Stars. In 2009, he formed the Paa Grant Soccer Academy to honour his grandfather.
🍫

Winfred Tete-Ansa

Cocoa Trader & Banker
Successful cocoa merchant — Gold Coast era
IndustryCocoa trade
Also didBanking
ExperienceEurope & America
MissionSupport African businesses

Ghana is one of the main producers of cocoa in the world — today, Ghana is the second largest cocoa exporter in the world. Entrepreneurs like Winfred Tete-Ansa helped make that possible.

He tried to get Congress to promote the cocoa-growing regions of Nigeria and the Gold Coast. He worked with 45 farmers' unions, but many farmers did not have enough money to get a higher price for their cocoa.

Tete-Ansa had lots of experience in banking and business in Europe and America. He opened a bank to support businesses that belonged to Africans, because European banks did not want to help them. Some of these businesses included those that traded in mahogany, palm oil, and other local items.

He understood that Africans needed their own bank to be truly independent. His idea helped many local business owners get the money they needed.
🍯

Dr. Esther Ocloo

Food Processing Pioneer & Women's Champion
Born: 18 April 1919 · One of Ghana's most well-known female entrepreneurs
CompanyNkulenu Industries Ltd
IndustryFood processing
Also didMicro-lending
Award (1990)African Prize for Leadership

Dr. Esther Afua Ocloo started Nkulenu Industries Limited. It is a well-known food processing company in Ghana. She left college because there were no jobs available due to World War II — but that did not stop her!

Her business soon grew into a very successful one. She then started a micro-lending business so that people could get small loans and grow their own businesses.

In 1976, Dr. Esther Ocloo, together with Michaela Walsh and Ela Bhatt, became founders of the Women's World Banking — an organisation that helped women around the world.

In 1990, she received the African Prize for Leadership and many other awards for her work in the economic empowerment of women and families.

Dr. Esther Ocloo showed that even during difficult times — like a World War — you can still find a way to start a business and help others. 💪
