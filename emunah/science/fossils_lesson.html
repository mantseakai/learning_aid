📖 What is a Fossil?

🦴 Definition

A fossil is an impression (a shape or mark) made in mud. When an animal or plant died long, long ago, the hard parts of its body got pressed into the mud. Over time, the mud hardened — and the shape of those hard parts got copied in the rock!

⏳ How does it happen?

Step 1 — An animal dies and sinks into soft mud.

Step 2 — The hard parts (like bones, shells, or teeth) press into the mud.

Step 3 — The mud slowly hardens into rock over millions of years.

Step 4 — The shape of the hard parts stays in the rock — that's a fossil! 🎉

💡 Fun Fact!

Fossils can be millions of years old! Scientists called palaeontologists study fossils to learn about animals and plants that lived before humans were even around! 🌍
